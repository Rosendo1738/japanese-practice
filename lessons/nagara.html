ながら

→ Go to ながら exercises

ながら is used to say that two actions happen at the same time.

One action is the background action, and the other is the main action.

Main action vs background action

The verb before ながら is the background action.

The verb after ながら is the main action.

The sentence focuses more on the main action.

Verb form

Use the ます-stem of the verb before ながら.

(See the Verb Stems lesson for how this form is made.)

Structure

[Verb A (ます-stem)] + ながら + [Verb B]

Verb A = background action
Verb B = main action

Examples

  • 音楽を聞きながら勉強する
    おんがくをききながらべんきょうする
    I study while listening to music.
  • テレビを見ながらごはんを食べる
    てれびをみながら ごはんをたべる
    I eat a meal while watching TV.
  • 先生の話を聞きながらノートを書く
    せんせいのはなしをききながら のーとをかく
    I write notes while listening to the teacher.

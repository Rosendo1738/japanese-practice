Verb Stems

A verb stem is a base part of a verb that is used before adding certain grammar patterns.

Some grammar rules require a verb stem instead of the full verb.

What is a verb stem?

The verb stem is the part of the verb that comes before ます.

You can think of it as:

verb + ます → remove ます → verb stem

Examples

  • 聞きます → 聞き
    ききます → きき
  • 読みます → 読み
    よみます → よみ
  • 食べます → 食べ
    たべます → たべ

Using the verb stem

When a grammar rule says:

“Use the verb stem”

it means to use this form of the verb before adding that grammar.

For example:

聞き + ながら
読み + ながら
食べ + ながら
